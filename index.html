Rotating Base

With an ambidextrous thumbscrew-operated mechanism, the low-profile rotating base mechanism allows the user to change the direction of the slider on the fly, no matter where they stand. Continuous 360° of motion means never running out of adjustment in any direction.

★

Horizontal Offset

A slider and a horizontal offset in one. Operated by a simple turn of the lead screw, using the provided crank or ¾” socket adapter for your hand drill, the entire base can be offset on the fly, even while the camera is still sitting on the slider. No locking mechanisms needed and zero slop throughout the entire length.

★

Steel Rollers

Using rails and linear bearing blocks usually found on high-end CNC machines means precise, smooth movements at any speed. With minimal resistance, a light push of a finger is all it takes to get the carriage rolling for those slow crawls. Completely silent at slow speeds; whisper-quiet when you need to go faster.

★

Quick Release Nut

A reimagining of the original castle nut design. No more expensive exposed threads catching on pieces of gear or cross-threaded nuts. A simple, heavy-duty bolt keeps everything locked down safely, while the redesigned nut allows the you easier access in the most awkward of spaces. Can be removed in less than a minute for extremely low-profile shots.

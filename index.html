Kinemat

Cinema Tech

From design, to prototyping, and production, Kinemat is a full-scale cinema gear manufacturer based in Vancouver, British Columbia. Drawing on many years of experience both in the film industry and design engineering, Dmytro Kolesnyk is the man behind the Kinemat K800 and K1200 Camera Slider. Kinemat's goal is simple - to build the most practical, durable, and innovative cinema gear for industry professionals at all scales of production.

Rotating Base

The K-model camera sliders' innovative rotating base allows the operator to change the direction of the slider instantly, no matter where they stand. The low-profile base is easily released via a thumbscrew mechanism, and allows for continous motion in 360°. With this mechanism, you never run out of adjustment in any direction.

Horizontal Offset

A slider and a horizontal offset in one. Operated by a simple turn of the lead screw, using the provided crank or ¾” socket adapter for your hand drill, the entire base can be offset on the fly, even while the camera is still sitting on the slider. No locking mechanisms needed and zero slop throughout the entire length.

Steel Rollers

Using rails and linear bearing blocks usually found on high-end CNC machines means precise, smooth movements at any speed. With minimal resistance, a light push of a finger is all it takes to get the carriage rolling for those slow crawls. Completely silent at slow speeds; whisper-quiet when you need to go faster.

Quick Release Nut

A reimagining of the original castle nut design. No more expensive exposed threads catching on pieces of gear or cross-threaded nuts. A simple, heavy-duty bolt keeps everything locked down safely, while the redesigned nut allows for easier access in the most awkward of spaces. Can be removed in less than a minute for extremely low-profile shots.
